ANDREW W. FISHER

Civil Engineering EIT — MASc Candidate

A Brief Background

My interest in civil engineering stems from my love of LEGO as a young child. Ever since, I have been fascinated with the "how" and "why" of structures; hoping to one day gain the knowledge and experience to design my own. Many years later, I'm now completing my Master's Degree in Civil Engineering at the University of Toronto, specializing in the design of concrete structures.

In my spare time I have been drawn to positions in student government including Vice-President Academic of my course union, member of the Graduate Students' Union Finance Committee, and Steward of CUPE 3902. I strive to be informed regarding issues that affect my student experience, and find the most effective way is to be directly involved with the decision making bodies.

I have also developed an interest in politics and public policy. I think the best way engineers can demonstrate our duty to society is to be directly involved in the decisions being made by our local government. I have taken steps to get involved in grass roots political campaigns and hope to one day be elected into my local government.

Industry Experience

Structural Engineering

Graduate Research Assistant at The University of Toronto

  • Designed, constructed, and tested to failure four full scale, high strength coupling beam wall assemblies
  • Investigated the reduction in shear capacity of coupling beams due to concrete cover spalling
  • Research findings to be used to develop clauses in the CSA A23.3-04 Design of Concrete Structures Standard to account for the reduced capacity

Bridge Rehabilitation

Bridge Research Assistant at the Ministry of Transportation - Ontario

  • Modeled highway bridges using S-FRAME and MIDAS software programs in order to analyze bridge responses under traffic loading
  • Participated in standardized load tests at provincial bridge sites to gather real world data to verify computer models
  • Aided in government research initiatives including load testing of corroded concrete beam specimens in Model Structures Laboratory

Wind Engineering

Technical Project Coordinator at RWDI Consulting Engineers

  • Coordinated over 45 cladding wind load and wind-induced structural load studies for residential and commercial buildings around the globe
  • Calculated design wind loads using data collected from boundary layer wind tunnel tests following international building code specifications for wind design
  • Submitted technical reports to the Project Manager, summarizing recommended structural and cladding wind loads for client use in the final design

Land Development

Concrete Design and Testing
