A Brief Background

My interest in civil engineering stems from my love of LEGO as a young child. Ever since, I have been fascinated with the "how" and "why" of structures; hoping to one day gain the knowledge and experience to design my own. Many years later, I have completed my Masters degree in civil engineering at the University of Toronto, specializing in the design of reinforced concrete structures, and am now working as a Strucutral Engineering Intern (EIT) for RJC Engineers, one of the top structural engineering firms in Canada.

In my spare time I have been drawn to volunteer leadership positions. In university, I was the Vice-President of my course union, Editor-in-Chief of my school newspaper, and Captain of a CSCE Concrete Toboggan Team, to name a few. I am currently volunteering with Professional Engineers Ontario (PEO) on the Government Liason Program (GLP) Committee.

I have also developed an interest in politics and public policy. I think the best way engineers can demonstrate our duty to society is to be directly involved in the decisions being made by our local government. I have taken steps to get involved in grass roots political campaigns and hope to one day be elected into my local government. I am currently a Director-at-Large with my local Federal riding association.
