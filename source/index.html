ANDREW W. FISHER

Civil Engineering EIT — MASc Candidate

A Brief Background

My interest in civil engineering stems from my love of LEGO as a young child. Ever since, I have been fascinated with the "how" and "why" of structures; hoping to one day gain the knowledge and experience to design my own. Many years later, I'm now completing my Master's Degree in Civil Engineering at the University of Toronto, specializing in design of concrete structures.

In my spare time I have been drawn to positions in student government including Vice-President Academic of our course union, member of the Graduate Students' Union Finance Committee, and Steward of CUPE 3902. I strive to be informed regarding issues that affect my student experience, and find the most effective way is to be directly involved with the decision making bodies.

I have also developed an interest in politics and public policy. I think the best way engineers can demonstrate our duty to society is to be directly involved in the decisions being made by our local government. I have taken steps to get involved in grass roots political campaigns and hope to one day be elected into my local government.

Career Interests

  • Structural Engineering

    Upon graduating I plan to be hired at a structural engineering consulting firm where I see myself joining a team who focuses on the design of concrete structures. I am looking for a team that will be able to mentor me for two years as an Engineering Intern (EIT), after which I can become an active P.Eng. within the firm.

  • Project Management

    While working as a P.Eng., I want to be involved in coordinating projects with clients, contractors, and government organizations. Long term, I want to become a Project Manager where I can oversee the technical design and coordination of multiple projects.

  • Technical Research

    I would like to join a consulting firm who chooses to push boundaries and investigate areas of structural design that may not have been thought a possibility in the past. I see technical research critical to the advancement of our industry, and hope to have a role in that advancement process.

  • Regulation & Public Policy

    Along side my engineering career, I plan on engaging with local politics in order to bring engineering insight to the decisions being made on behalf of my community. I would like hold a seat on my city’s Public Works and Infrastructure Committee or equivalent. My long term goal is to run for a city councilor position.
