Civil Engineering EIT — MASc Candidate

A Brief Background

My interest in civil engineering stems from my love of LEGO as a young child. Ever since, I have been fascinated with the engineering and design decisions that bring a structure to fruition. Fueling this passion is my attraction to project management where I can be a key contributor in taking an engineering design from start to finish.

I have also developed an interest in politics and public policy. I think the best way engineers can demonstrate our duty to society is to be directly involved in the decisions being made by our local government. The more input engineers can bring to politics, the more effective our government will be.

Career Interests

  • Structural Engineering

    Upon graduating I plan to be hired at a structural engineering consulting firm. I would like to join a design team who has a focus on concrete structures and will be able to mentor me for two years as an Engineering Intern (EIT), after which I can become an active P.Eng. within the company.

  • Project Management

    While working as a P.Eng., I hope to be involved in the management of projects which include interacting with clients and government organizations. In the future, I want to become a Project Manager where i can oversee the technical design and coordination of assigned projects.

  • Technical Research

    I would like to join a consulting firm who chooses to push boundaries and investigate areas of structural design that may not have been thought a possibility in the past. I see technical research critical to the advancement of our industry, and hope to have a minor role in that advancement process.

  • Regulation & Public Policy

    Along side my engineering career, I plan on engaging with local politics in order to bring engineering insight to the decisions being made on behalf of my community. I would like hold a seat on my city’s Public Works and Infrastructure Committee (or equivalent). My long term goal is to run for a city councilor position.
